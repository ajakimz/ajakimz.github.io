Critcal Reflection & Analysis

The design of my Movie Club website was guided by the specific needs of the local movie community. Using CSS Grid, I created distinct layouts for the featured movies and upcoming screenings pages, organizing content to prioritize clarity and accessibility. The featured movies page allows users to browse and filter films by genre, while the upcoming screenings page highlights events with clear headings, a selectable list, and feedback. These grids provide a logical reading flow, making it easier for users to find relevant information and take action without being overwhelmed. Accessibility considerations, such as keyboard navigation, aria-live feedback, and high-contrast selections, ensure the site is inclusive for users with different abilities.

The interactive JavaScript features were carefully chosen to serve community needs rather than for visual flair. The genre filter on the featured movies page helps users quickly find content relevant to their interests, and the selectable upcoming screenings list provides immediate feedback while allowing deselection. User testing revealed that spacing, selection feedback, and clarity of interactive elements were critical, leading to adjustments that improved usability and visual clarity. Feedback also challenged my initial assumptions about subtle design cues, showing that clear, unambiguous interactions are more effective in a community-focused site. Given more time, I would explore additional personalization features, such as saving selections or notifications, while maintaining accessibility and usability.

Privacy and user agency were key considerations throughout development. No personal user data is stored; all interactions occur locally, giving users full control over filtering and selecting screenings. The design also acknowledges potential biases, such as genre selection or the order of events, and highlights the importance of representing diverse community interests in future iterations. Overall, the project demonstrates how thoughtful technical decisions (like grid layouts, interactive functionality, and accessibility) can create a meaningful and usable experience, emphasizing that web development should prioritize serving real community needs while maintaining user trust and empowerment.
