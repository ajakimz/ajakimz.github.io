Overview

Universal Design (UD) is the approach of creating products, environments, and digital interfaces that are usable by the widest range of people regardless of ability, age, experience level, or context. It focuses on inclusivity and accessibility at the core of the design process.

Key Concepts

  • The 7 Principles of Universal Design: Equitable use, flexibility in use, simple and intuitive use, perceptible information, tolerance for error, low physical effort, and appropriate size and space for use.
  • Diversity of Web Users: People vary in physical ability, sensory ability, cognitive styles, language proficiency, and device usage.
  • User Profiles & Disability Demographics: Designers must consider real-world usage patterns and accessibility needs based on population data.
